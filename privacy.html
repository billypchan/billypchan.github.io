Privacy Policy — 周易占卜 (I Ching)

Last updated: 13 May 2026 · Effective for the Android app under package com.billypchan.iching and the iOS counterpart.
Data controller: Bill Chan (individual developer), contact: billypchan@gmail.com.

This page describes what data the 周易占卜 (I Ching) mobile app collects, how it is used, and what choices you have. The app is published by Bill Chan as an individual developer.

1. What we collect

On your device only (never sent to us):

  • Divination history — the timestamp, hexagram, and (if you grant the permission) approximate location of each divination you cast. Stored in the app's local database. You can delete history at any time from inside the app, and it is removed when you uninstall.
  • Settings — your text-to-speech language preference, your GDPR consent choices (the IAB TCF transparency & consent string, stored in IABTCF_* keys), and similar preferences. Stored locally.

Sent to third parties:

  • Advertising identifier (Android Advertising ID / iOS IDFA) and standard ad-request metadata are collected by Google AdMob to serve banner ads inside the app. Google's handling of this data is governed by the Google Privacy Policy and "How Google uses information from sites or apps that use our services". You can reset or limit your advertising ID from your device settings.

What we do not collect: name, email, phone number, contacts, photos, microphone audio, calendar, files, account login, payment details. The app has no sign-up flow.

2. Why we use the location permission

The app requests the optional ACCESS_COARSE_LOCATION / ACCESS_FINE_LOCATION permission so it can record where you cast a divination, alongside the time. This is part of traditional divination practice and is stored only in your local divination history. The location is never transmitted off your device, never used for advertising, and never shared with any third party. Denying the permission has no effect on the rest of the app.

3. Ads and the IAB TCF consent framework

The free version of the app shows banner ads served by Google AdMob. Ads are not personalised based on data we hold (we don't hold any), but Google may personalise them based on data Google itself has collected.

For users in the European Economic Area, the United Kingdom, and Switzerland, the AdMob SDK presents a Google-provided consent form on first launch, built on the IAB Transparency & Consent Framework (TCF) v2.2. The form asks you to consent to (or object to) the following processing purposes:

  • Store and/or access information on the device (e.g. cookies, advertising ID).
  • Select basic ads.
  • Create a personalised ads profile and select personalised ads.
  • Measure ad performance.
  • Apply market research to generate audience insights.
  • Develop and improve products.

Your choices are saved on the device as an IAB TCF "TC string" (IABTCF_TCString) and passed to ad partners with every ad request.

Advertising partners

We work with one TCF vendor:

  • Google (Google Ireland Limited / Google LLC) — ad serving via AdMob, ad measurement, audience insights. See Google's vendor entry on the AdMob list of advertising technology providers and the IAB TCF Global Vendor List.

Legal basis (GDPR)

  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR) for personalised advertising and for storing/accessing information on your device for ad purposes.
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR) for limited, non-personalised ads and basic ad measurement when you select the "Manage options" path and decline personalisation.

International transfers

Data shared with Google may be transferred to the United States and other countries where Google operates infrastructure. Such transfers rely on the EU Standard Contractual Clauses and Google's supplementary safeguards, as described in the Google Privacy Policy.

4. Children

The app is not directed at children under 13 (or the equivalent local minimum age). We do not knowingly collect data from children. The Play Store target-audience setting is 13+.

5. Data retention

All data collected on your device stays on your device for as long as the app is installed. Uninstalling the app deletes all local data, including your stored TCF consent string. Data sent to Google AdMob is retained according to Google's policies.

6. Your choices

  • Re-open the consent form at any time from the app's Settings → Privacy options button to change or withdraw your GDPR consent. (This uses Google UMP's presentPrivacyOptionsForm.)
  • Deny or revoke the location permission from system settings.
  • Reset or limit your advertising identifier from system settings (iOS: Settings → Privacy & Security → Tracking / Apple Advertising; Android: Settings → Privacy → Ads).
  • Delete divination history from inside the app.
  • Uninstall the app to remove all local data.

7. Your GDPR rights

If you are in the EEA, UK, or Switzerland, you have the right to:

  • access the personal data we hold about you;
  • request rectification of inaccurate data;
  • request erasure of your data ("right to be forgotten");
  • request restriction of processing;
  • object to processing based on legitimate interests;
  • data portability;
  • withdraw consent at any time (use the in-app Privacy options button, or reinstall the app);
  • lodge a complaint with your local data protection supervisory authority.

To exercise any of these rights, email billypchan@gmail.com. Note that, because we do not hold any data about you on our own servers, most requests will be answered by directing you to the in-app or device controls listed above, or by pointing you at Google's data-subject request channels for AdMob-collected data.

8. Changes to this policy

If we make a material change we will update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page. Continued use of the app after a change constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.

9. Contact

Questions, concerns, or data requests: billypchan@gmail.com
